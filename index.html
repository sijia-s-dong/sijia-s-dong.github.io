About Me

I am a postdoctoral scholar in the Center for Molecular Engineering at Argonne National Laboratory working on using machine learning techniques to accelerate first-principles-based simulations of condensed matter. Prior to Argonne, I was a postdoctoral scholar at the University of Minnesota focusing on using and developing electronic structure theory methods to study the excited states of molecules that are building blocks of large biomolecules and photovoltaic systems. I obtained my Ph.D. in chemistry from Caltech in 2017, where I worked with Prof. William A. Goddard III to develop first-principles-based and data-driven computational methods to understand and predict various processes in nature, from electron dynamics in warm dense hydrogen to conformational changes in proteins important to human health.

I am particularly interested in multiscale processes. A major theme of my research is to develop efficient first-principles-based and data-driven computational methods to predict and explore the ground- and excited-state energy surfaces of large molecular systems and complex materials. My goal is to understand and design molecules, materials, and processes for renewable energy and biomedical applications.

I will be joining the Department of Chemistry and Chemical Biology at Northeastern University as an assistant professor in January 2021.

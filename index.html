About Me

I am a postdoctoral scholar in Prof. Donald G. Truhlar's group at University of Minnesota. I use various electronic structure theory methods and develop analysis tools to study excited states in biomolecules and functional materials. I obtained my Ph.D. in chemistry from Caltech in 2017, where I worked with Prof. William A. Goddard III to develop first-principles-based computational and theoretical methods to understand and predict various processes in nature, from electron dynamics in warm dense hydrogen to conformational changes in proteins important to human health.

A major theme of my research is to develop efficient methods to explore the ground and excited state energy surfaces of large molecular systems. I also apply various computational methods to understand and design molecules, materials, and processes for energy and biomedical applications.

About

I will be joining the Department of Chemistry and Chemical Biology at Northeastern University as an assistant professor in January 2021. Please navigate to the Research and Openings pages for more information about the research directions and open positions of my lab at Northeastern.

I am passionate about using computation and automation to accelerate science. As a postdoctoral scholar in the Center for Molecular Engineering at Argonne National Laboratory, I use machine learning techniques to accelerate first-principles-based simulations of condensed matter. Prior to Argonne, I was a postdoctoral scholar at the University of Minnesota focusing on using and developing electronic structure theory methods to study the excited states of molecules that are building blocks of large biomolecules and photovoltaic systems. I obtained my Ph.D. in chemistry from Caltech in 2017, where I worked with Prof. William A. Goddard III to develop first-principles-based and data-driven computational methods to understand and predict various processes in nature, from electron dynamics in warm dense hydrogen to conformational changes in proteins important to human health.

I am particularly interested in multiscale processes. The major theme of my PhD and postdoctoral research is to develop efficient physics-based and data-driven computational methods to predict and explore the ground- and excited-state energy surfaces and other quantum mechanical properties of large molecular systems and complex materials. My goal is to understand and design molecules, materials, and processes for renewable energy, biomedicine, and quantum information science.
